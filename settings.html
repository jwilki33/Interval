Switch to Active Tracking?

Active mode replaces manual flagging with heart rate monitoring. Here's what that means.

What data is used

BPM readings are collected from your Bluetooth heart rate monitor and stored locally on this device. Nothing is sent to any server.

How focus is calculated

A calm, steady heart rate signals deep focus. When your HR rises more than 15 bpm above your rolling baseline and holds there for 10+ seconds, the stability curve registers it as a disruption — automatically.

Linking a device

Open the Tracker, then press Connect HR. Your browser scans for nearby devices — select yours and wait for the green indicator. Compatible with Polar H10, Garmin HRM-Pro, Wahoo TICKR, Fitbit Charge 5+, and any standard Bluetooth HR monitor.

Requires Chrome or Edge with Bluetooth enabled. Spike detection activates after a 2-minute warmup.
